Network & the 51% Attack

No one is in charge of Bitcoin. Every node simply keeps the chain with the most work. Two things follow: the network sometimes forks and heals itself — and an attacker with enough hashpower can secretly out-build the honest chain to reverse a payment that already looked confirmed.

Things to notice

Confirmations buy safety

Each block mined on top of a payment makes reversing it exponentially harder — for any attacker below 50%. That's the whole reason to "wait for 6 confirmations".

51% isn't a magic switch

Even just under half the hashpower gives a real chance of a double-spend; just over half makes it a near-certainty given patience. Security degrades smoothly, then collapses.

Honesty is the default win

Mining honestly on the longest chain is the most profitable strategy for anyone without a majority — which is exactly why the network stays converged.
