The HD Wallet

A modern wallet isn't a pile of keys — it's one seed phrase. Twelve words become a 512-bit seed (BIP-39), and that seed deterministically grows an entire tree of keys and addresses (BIP-32). Back up the phrase once and you've backed up every address you'll ever use.

Things to notice

Deterministic, not random

The same phrase always rebuilds the exact same tree, in any conforming wallet. Lose your device, type the words into a new one, and every address comes back.

The passphrase is a hidden wallet

Add a passphrase and you get a completely different tree from the same words — a plausible-deniability vault. Forget it and that money is gone forever.

Hardened keeps branches private

Hardened steps stop someone who holds one account's public key from deriving its siblings. That's why account-level paths use the apostrophe.
