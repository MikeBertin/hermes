Multisig Vault

A single private key is a single point of failure: lose it and the coins are gone; let it leak and they're stolen. A multisig vault fixes both. Three cosigners each hold a key, but the coins live at one address governed by a rule — 2 of 3 must sign. Below is a real P2WSH vault, its address built by hand from the three keys. Why treasuries hold coins this way

Lose a key, keep your coins

A 2-of-3 vault survives one lost or destroyed key — the other two still spend. No single backup whose loss is fatal.

Steal a key, steal nothing

An attacker who compromises one cosigner gets one signature — not enough. The threshold turns a catastrophic breach into a recoverable one (rotate the lost key).

One address, many guards

The keys can sit in different places — an office, a vault, a lawyer — yet the world sends to a single bc1q… address. This is exactly how corporate Bitcoin custody is built.
